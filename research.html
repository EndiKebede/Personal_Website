Research agenda

My research advances data-driven sustainability science at the intersection of water, food, and energy. I focus on building open datasets and analytical approaches that help quantify how irrigation and water scarcity reshape freshwater resources, agricultural production, and food supply systems.

Theme 1 — Develop and leverage large-scale environmental datasets

Many urgent sustainability questions cannot be answered without consistent, high-resolution, and open data. I develop datasets that integrate remote sensing, agricultural statistics, and reproducible workflows to enable global-to-local assessments of irrigation, cropping patterns, and food-system risks.

  • MIRCA-OS: monthly irrigated and rainfed cropped areas for the 21st century.
  • AEI: global area equipped for irrigation (infrastructure-focused perspective).
  • High-resolution irrigation mapping using combined remote sensing + machine learning.

Theme 2 — Evolution of global and local water scarcity and its impacts on food production & supply

Water scarcity is changing in space and time due to climate variability, groundwater depletion, infrastructure, and socioeconomic constraints. I quantify how scarcity evolves from global patterns to local hotspots and assess how these shifts translate into risks for food production, stability, and supply.

  • Global and local risk assessments linking water scarcity to production and supply outcomes.
  • Drivers of persistent economic water scarcity and implications for adaptation and investment.

Theme 3 — Competition for water between sectors

Water allocation is increasingly shaped by competing demands from agriculture, cities, energy systems, and ecosystems. I study cross-sector competition and trade-offs to identify where water conflicts may intensify and where policy and management can improve resilience under scarcity.

  • Quantifying trade-offs among agricultural, domestic/urban, industrial, and environmental water needs.
  • Identifying regions where scarcity-driven competition may reshape food–water outcomes.
